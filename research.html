Research Projects

Our research primarily focuses on Interpretable Fault Diagnosis and Control with Formal Methods and its applications in Complex Electromechanical Systems:

  • Interpretable Fault Diagnosis
  • Control Synthesis with Temporal Logic
  • Robot Control with Machine Learning
  • Intelligent Task Scheduling and Planning
  • Intelligent Signal Processing
  • Inverse Dynamics Problem
    • Interpretable Fault Diagnosis aims to design diagnostic systems that not only identify faults but also provide human-understandable explanations for their predictions. Our work spans efficient construction of sparse temporal logic explanations, graph-based diagnostic reasoning, and real-time implementation in complex systems.
    • Control Synthesis with Temporal Logic focuses on designing control strategies guided by temporal logic specifications to ensure system behavior adheres to desired constraints. Our research includes automating the synthesis of temporal logic-based controllers, integrating symbolic reasoning with machine learning, and validating control strategies in real-world robotic systems.
    • Robot Control with Machine Learning aims to enhance the adaptability and robustness of robotic systems through data-driven control strategies. Our work involves reinforcement learning for robot motion planning, imitation learning for human-like coordination, and multi-modal perception integration for dynamic environments.
    • Intelligent Task Scheduling and Planning focuses on optimizing resource allocation and task execution in complex systems. Our research includes the development of real-time task scheduling algorithms, probabilistic planning in uncertain environments, and adaptive decision-making frameworks for large-scale operations.
    • Intelligent Signal Processing aims to analyze and interpret signals in complex environments through advanced processing techniques. Our work includes developing enhanced instantaneous frequency extraction methods, adaptive filtering for noisy data, and interpretable machine learning models for signal analysis.
    • Inverse Dynamics Problem focuses on estimating forces and torques required for desired motion trajectories in complex mechanical systems. Our research includes developing efficient numerical solvers, leveraging machine learning for inverse dynamics estimation, and ensuring real-time applicability in robotic systems.
